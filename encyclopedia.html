Metaphor

A direct comparison that lets one thing speak as another — no "like," no "as," just identity asserted between two unlike things.

Definition

A metaphor names one thing as if it were another, asking the reader to carry the qualities of the second thing onto the first. Where a simile says something is like something else, a metaphor simply says it is.

History

Aristotle called metaphor "the greatest thing by far" a poet could master — the ability to see resemblance between dissimilar things. It's the oldest device in the encyclopedia, present in the earliest surviving verse in every language Verso has catalogued.

Examples in the wild

"Hope is the thing with feathers—
that perches in the soul—"
Emily Dickinson, on hope as a bird that never leaves
